Descriptive Effects Pedal

Audio Engineering Made Simple

Overview

What's the problem?

Most Effects Pedals for electronic instruments are made for experienced musicians who what a specific sound and effect. The musician must know ahead of time what each pedal does. Someone who does not understand sound design and music theory would need to conduct research into each pedal before buying. The effects used in the effects pedal change the tone from how the instrument sounds normally, referred to as clean by guitarists, into a new effect dictated by what the effects pedal does. For example, an electric guitarist could use a distortion pedal, which makes the guitar sound louder and messier. This sound can be heard in most rock, metal, and other genres of music. The pedal aspect of each device refers to how guitarists will interact with the device by pushing a button which turns the effect on and off.

Our goal

The Descriptive Effects Pedal, DEP for short, is a new effect pedal for electronic instruments that uses descriptions of musical sounds to change the output signal of the connected instrument. The DEP will be primarily used with instruments who are electric by nature such as an electric guitar or an electronic keyboard. The device serves to aid those who are aspiring to learn about effects for their instrument, without any sound design or music theory background. The DEP will allow a user to describe the sound of the instrument they want to create by selecting pre-programmed descriptions. These descriptions correspond to digital effects that will take the input sound of the connected instrument and shape it into a sound in which they describe. This sound can either be played through the on-board speakers, or though other connected devices such as a speaker or headphones.

Specifications

Components

  • Touch Screen
  • 1/4in and 1/8in Input Jack
  • 1/4in and 1/8in Output Jack
  • On-Board Speaker

Functionality

  • 3 Active Audio Effects
  • 7 Total Different Audio Effects
  • Up to 3 Parameters for each Effect
  • Automatically switch between Speaker and Output Jack

    How does it work?

    The DEP runs using a LPC55S69 microcontroller. The user will interract with a touch screen on the board which is controlled using C code running on the microcontroller. This allows us to program the algorithms that will process the audio waveform that the connected instrument provides.

    Embedded Wizard

    We are using a frontend software that allows us to design the LCD screen's desplay using a visual approach. This then generates code for us that can be imported to our own projects where we can use our own program and functions.

    Hardware

    Audio Processing

    We have Operational Amplifier circuits that will pre-process the audio signal before it is read by the Analog-to-Digital Converter that is on the LPC55S69. Additionally we have post-processing Operational Amplifier circuits that processes the signal output from the Digital-to-Analog Converter.
